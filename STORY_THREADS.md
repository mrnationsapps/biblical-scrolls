# BibleFeed — Story Threads & Drama Hooks

> A living catalog of the narrative arcs, rivalries, scandals, and emotional beats we
> can latch onto when generating posts, comment threads, news reports, and memorials.
> Add to this freely as we build the roster era by era. Nothing here is invented
> outside Scripture — these are real biblical dramas we surface in a social-feed voice.

---

## Recurring app mechanics these threads feed
- **Trickle-in drama** — the spicy comments arrive *after* the post (hours/days later),
  so a fight unfolds over time (§4.6 / §6.1 of DESIGN.md).
- **News-outlet coverage** — big events get "reported" by the 3–4 outlets, and
  characters argue in the comments.
- **Memorial pages** — deaths trigger an obituary post + condolences + a memorial wall.
- **Friend-graph reveals** — browsing a character's friends list exposes alliances and
  feuds (who's NOT friends with whom is its own drama).
- **"People You May Know"** — a new character entering a tense situation surfaces right
  as the conflict heats up.

## Keeping embers alive — seeding & watering long arcs ⭐
A payoff only lands if the reader has *stake* in it. So we don't just plant a seed and wait
1,000 biblical years — we **water** it: occasional, organic references that keep the ember
warm, so the resolution feels *earned*, not arbitrary. (Amalek→Haman is the proof of concept;
the Messiah is the supreme case.)

**Feed-native ways to water an ember** (vary them — never a "PREVIOUSLY ON…" drumbeat):
- **Ambient NPC posts/comments** — a minor character offhandedly references the old hope or
  grudge ("Grandfather never let us forget what Amalek did to us in the desert").
- **News-outlet throwbacks** — anniversary/retrospective pieces ("Generations ago today,
  Samuel struck down King Agag…").
- **"On This Day" memories (§4.11)** — resurfaces the original seed post itself; the watering
  mechanism is *already built into the app*.
- **Festivals & observances** — Passover re-lights the Exodus ember every cycle, Purim will
  re-light Esther; the calendar waters for free.
- **Prophets posting** — re-announcing unresolved promises is literally their role; keeps the
  messianic embers glowing (stay scripturally exact — real prophecy only).
- **Genealogy breadcrumbs** — a birth in the messianic line gets a quiet nod ("another
  generation in the line of Judah — the scepter has not departed").
- **Profile bios / life-events** — carry a grudge or a hope forward across generations.

**The killer reuse — personalized watering.** The engagement algorithm (§5.6 / §7.3) waters
the threads *each user cares about*: follow Agag, react to Samuel's confrontation, and your
feed gets more Amalek embers — so when Haman falls it hits *you* hardest, because *you* tended
that fire. The "show you more of what you like" engine doubles as a **personal foreshadowing
engine**; stake becomes personal.

**Guardrails:** keep it *occasional and organic* — over-watering nags and spoils the surprise
(anti-doomscroll, §4.9). When watering prophecy, stay scripturally exact: prophets quote real
prophecy; NPC chatter may voice hope or longing but never invents it.
