# BibleFeed — Post Formats Catalog

> Reusable post *templates* the content engine draws on to keep the feed varied and
> Facebook-true. Distinct from `STORY_THREADS.md` (specific one-time arcs) and
> `DESIGN.md` (architecture). When generating a Beat's content, mix these formats so
> the feed feels like a real, living social network — not a chronological summary.

---

## Format 1 — The Dilemma / "Should I do this?" post ⭐
A character faces a real decision and crowdsources it from their friends, exactly like
the "should I?? 🤔" posts on real Facebook. Friends weigh in across the comments — some
for, some against, sometimes arguing with each other.

**Why it's uniquely powerful here:** *dramatic irony.* On real Facebook nobody knows the
outcome; on BibleFeed the **reader knows how it ends**, so an innocent "should I?" post
becomes suspense. The reader is yelling at the screen while the commenters have no idea.
Real social media literally cannot do this — it's a superpower of the chronological concept.

**Mechanics:**
- Post type = *advice-seeking / dilemma.* Often a **Spine** moment (a real decision point)
  dressed up as casual texture.
- Comments **trickle in** (per DESIGN §4.6) and **split pro/con**; friends may argue.
- **Voice-tuned by age/era:** young characters → "girl, NO 🚩"; elders → measured counsel.
- The **🙏 Pray reaction** fits these perfectly.
- ⭐ **Comments that age badly + a late follow-up:** a friend cheers the bad choice on the
  post, then — *days later, after it goes wrong* — adds a second comment underneath:
  "…I'm so sorry, I never should have pushed you toward this." Devastatingly real, and it
  rewards the user for revisiting an old post.
- ⭐ **Performs live (DESIGN §4.6 live reveal):** react to the dilemma and watch the friends
  pile into the comments in real time — "… is typing" → "don't do it" → "… is typing" →
  "do what you must, sis" — taking sides and arguing with each other as you read. The
  drama unfolds in front of you because you engaged.

**Seed examples** (all straight from Scripture):
| Poster | The dilemma | The split in the comments | The irony the reader feels |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sarai | give Hagar to Abram to build a family? | "do what you must" / "don't, sis" | echoes for millennia |
| Esau | trade the birthright for a bowl of stew? | "bro NO" / "eat, you earned it" | selling his destiny for soup |
| Lot | take the rich grazing land by Sodom? | "great deal!" / "that city's bad news" | he loses everything there |
| David (cave) | strike the sleeping Saul? | Abishai: "YES" / "he's the LORD's anointed" | we know he spares him |
| Samson | marry the Philistine woman? | parents: "none of our own people??" | it ends in fire |
| Balaam | go curse Israel for the king's money? | "easy money" / "is that allowed?" | the donkey sees more |
| Rehoboam | lower taxes (elders) or flex (young friends)? | two camps at war | he splits the kingdom |

## Formats still to catalog (stubs — flesh out as ideas come)
- **Daily-life / lifestyle** — meals, work, what they wore, the pet they got, kids' antics.
- **Humble-brag / flex** — the boastful post (Lamech, Nimrod, Goliath's taunts).
- **Vague-posting** — cryptic, ominous one-liners ("big changes coming 👀") before a beat.
- **Throwback / "On This Day"** — resurfacing a past biblical moment (DESIGN §4.10).
- **Milestone / Life Event** — marriages, births, moves, new jobs (DESIGN §4.10).
- **Memorial / RIP** — death posts + condolences + the memorial wall (DESIGN §4.10).
- **News report** — the auto-followed outlets covering Spine beats (DESIGN §4.7, §5.5).
- **Ad** — period-true, comedic sponsored slots (DESIGN §4.8).
- **Check-in / location** — "—at the Jordan River 📍" travel/journey posts.
- **Relationship-status change** — the engagement/breakup/"it's complicated" drama post.
