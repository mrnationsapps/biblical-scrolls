## What Beat 003 proves (especially "the world with more people")
- **A populated world, grieving together** — ~10+ souls now; reactions are small-community scale
  ("Sareth, Lirah and 4 others"); the memorial wall fills with *named relatives*. **This is the
  answer to the population question — and Cain's own "whoever finds me" fear (Gen 4:14) is
  Scripture's in-text proof of it.**
- **The first MEMORIAL (§4.11)** — Abel's profile becomes a memorial wall; the family posts
  condolences ("gone too soon," "the gentlest hands," "we miss you"). The grief mechanic, fully
  demonstrated, with a community instead of a couple.
- **Violence referenced, never depicted (§6.5)** — the murder happens off-screen; the horror is
  carried entirely by Abel's feed *going silent.* The 12+ rating, honored.
- **The `Inferred` tier in action (Gen 5:4)** — the siblings *exist* canonically; their names and
  voices are inferred. Never contradicts Scripture, never borrows extra-biblical names.
- **The daily rhythm** — morning offerings → the dread of "the seventh hour" → the "cool of the
  day" confrontation → the exile at "the first watch of the night."
- **Ember continuity** — Gen 3:15 carried through the grief; Eve's hope pivots from Abel to the
  coming Seth.
- **The "aged-badly" comment** — Lirah's cheerful "go talk it out, I'm glad" curdles into anguished
  regret on the memorial wall hours later.
